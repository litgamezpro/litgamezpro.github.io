How to Play Breakout


Breakout is easy to start but tough to master. The goal is simple: use your paddle to bounce the ball and break all the bricks on the screen. If the ball gets past your paddle, you lose a life. Once you run out of lives, it’s game over. As you clear the bricks, you progress through increasingly challenging levels.



Power-ups fall from broken bricks and offer special abilities that can change the tide of the game. These include:




  • Adding multiple balls to the screen

  • Widening your paddle for better control

  • Making the ball “heavy” to smash through multiple bricks

  • Equipping your paddle with lasers for direct attacks



Tip: Picking up a new power-up will replace your current one. Be strategic—sometimes it’s better to avoid new power-ups if you’re doing well.

The Legacy of Breakout



Originally developed by Atari in 1976, Breakout was designed by Nolan Bushnell and Steve Bristow and built by tech legends Steve Wozniak and Steve Jobs. The game became an instant hit and inspired countless versions across platforms—from the Atari 2600 to hidden iPod Easter eggs.
